1 · Overview

Vega Assisted Living Fund II, L.P. pays monthly distributions to all limited partners. Distributions are classified as Preferred Return. This SOP covers the full cycle: preparing payment files, executing payments through Mountain America Credit Union (MACU), second-party review, logging in Syndication Pro, updating the Distribution Master Sheet, and submitting DTCC AIP activity records for specified custody investors.

1.1 Distribution Date

Distributions are sent on the first business day of each month. If the first of the month falls on a weekend or federal holiday, distributions are sent on the next business day. Payment is initiated and submitted by Maker on the distribution date. Wire and ACH cut-off times at MACU must be observed.

1.2 Payment Methods
MethodPlatformUsed For
ACH MACU Business ACH Majority of investors (direct bank accounts)
Wire MACU Business Wires Custodied investors (Schwab/Inspira) and others requiring wire delivery
Check / Billpay MACU Billpay Investors receiving physical check (currently: Ben Nelson / 2245 Holdings LLC)
1.3 Source of Truth

The Distribution Master Sheet (Google Drive) is the source of truth for all investor payment details: amounts, routing numbers, account numbers, entity names, and FFC references. Each month has its own tab. Always work from the current month's tab. Verify amounts against subscription documents before sending if any investor's amount has changed.

7 · Syndication Pro: Log Distributions

After payments are submitted and reviewed, log the distribution in Syndication Pro so it appears on investor reporting. This step uses a CSV import. The sample CSV template is downloaded directly from Syndication Pro (Import Distributions dialog). A copy of the blank sample file and each month's final submitted file are maintained in Google Drive under Phase 3 — Distributions for reference.

Important: Syndication Pro has three distribution action buttons. You must use Publish only. Do not click "Publish and Notify" (sends emails to investors prematurely) and do not click "Proceed to Payments" (payments are handled in MACU, not Syndication Pro).

8 · DTCC AIP: Activity & Distribution Reporting

For investors custodied at Charles Schwab, each distribution must also be reported to Schwab via a DTCC AIP Activity & Distribution record (Record Type 050). This is in addition to, not instead of, the wire payment. Refer to DTCC-SOP-001 for the full DTCC AIP reporting process. Note: DTCC-SOP-001 is currently being updated to reflect current platform formats and will be re-uploaded to Drive.
