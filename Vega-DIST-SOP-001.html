1. Overview

Vega Assisted Living Fund II, L.P. pays monthly distributions to all limited partners. Distributions are classified as Preferred Return. This SOP covers the full cycle: preparing payment files, executing payments through Mountain America Credit Union (MACU), second-party review, logging in Syndication Pro, updating the Distribution Master Sheet, and submitting DTCC AIP activity records for specified custody investors.

1.1 Distribution Date

Distributions are sent on the first business day of each month. If the first of the month falls on a weekend or federal holiday, distributions are sent on the next business day. Payment is initiated and submitted by Maker on the distribution date. Wire and ACH cut-off times at MACU must be observed.

1.3 Source of Truth

The Distribution Master Sheet is the source of truth for all investor payment details: amounts, routing numbers, account numbers, entity names, and FFC references. Each month has its own tab. Always work from the current month's tab. Verify amounts against subscription documents before sending if any investor's amount has changed.

2. Preparation

PHASE 1 Confirm Amounts & Prepare Master Sheet Complete before distribution date
StepActionRole
01 Open the Distribution Master Sheet and create or verify the current month's tab Naming convention: "[Month] [YYYY]" (e.g., "April 2026"). Copy the prior month's tab as a starting point. Do not delete prior month tabs. They are the historical record. Maker
02 Confirm distribution amounts for all investors Amounts are based on each investor's committed capital at the applicable preferred return rate. For new investors, apply the cutoff rule: if the investor funded before the 15th of the month, include them in that month's distribution. If they funded on or after the 15th, their first distribution begins the following month. Verify all new investor amounts against their subscription documents before populating the sheet. Maker
03 Verify all bank details are current: routing numbers, account numbers, entity names, FFC references for wire investors Do not assume prior month details are correct. For new investors, pull banking information directly from their subscription documents and confirm with them prior to their first distribution. Maker
04 Confirm the addenda line at the bottom of the tab matches the current month Format: VEGA ALF II - [Month] [Year] Distribution (e.g., "VEGA ALF II - Apr 2026 Distribution"). This is used as the ACH company entry description and the Syndication Pro memo. Maker

4. Wire Payments

Navigate to Business Wires in MACU online banking. Wires are sent individually, one wire per investor. Current wire investors are listed in the Wires section of the Distribution Master Sheet.

Wire cut-off: MACU wire cut-off is 4:00 PM Mountain Standard Time. Wires submitted after this time will process on the next business day. Submit wires early on distribution day.

6. Checker Review

PHASE 5 Second-Party Review Margaret McCann, margaret@vegarei.com, or designated reviewer

After all payments are submitted, the Checker independently reviews all submissions in the MACU portal before they process. This is a hard control. No month's distribution is considered complete without Checker sign-off.

StepActionRole
01 Notify Checker that all payments have been submitted and are ready for review Provide Checker with the current month's Distribution Master Sheet tab and MACU portal access if needed. Maker
02 Review ACH batch in MACU: verify all payees, amounts, and account numbers against the Master Sheet Check: correct payee name, correct account number, correct amount, correct send date. Checker
03 Review each wire under Business Wires — Scheduled: verify payee, amount, wire purpose, FFC reference, and send date Checker
04 Verify Billpay check amount and payee Checker
05 Confirm total of all payments matches the expected total distribution amount Expected total = sum of all investor amounts in the current month's Master Sheet tab. Checker
06 If any discrepancy is found, notify Maker immediately before payments process Payments can be cancelled in MACU if caught before the processing cut-off. Do not allow payments to process with unresolved discrepancies. Checker
07 Confirm review is complete to Maker Checker

7. Syndication Pro: Log Distributions

After payments are submitted and reviewed, log the distribution in Syndication Pro so it appears on investor reporting. This step uses a CSV import. The sample CSV template is downloaded directly from Syndication Pro. A copy of the blank sample file and each month's final submitted file are maintained in Google Drive under Phase 3 — Distributions for reference.

Important: Syndication Pro has three action buttons. You must use Publish only. Do not click "Publish and Notify" (sends emails to investors). Do not click "Proceed to Payments" (payments are handled in MACU, not Syndication Pro).
PHASE 6 Log in Syndication Pro vega.invportal.com/admin/dashboard
StepActionRole
01 Download the sample CSV from Syndication Pro or open the prior month's submitted file from Google Drive (Phase 3 — Distributions) In Syndication Pro: Portfolio — Vega Assisted Living Fund II — Distributions — Add Distribution — Download Sample File. Amount: Each investor's distribution amount from the Master Sheet
Start Date / End Date / Payment Date: Distribution date in format MMM-DD-YYYY (e.g., Apr-01-2026)
Distribution Type: Preferred Return (must match exactly)
All other columns remain as-is from the template. 		Maker

For investors custodied at Charles Schwab, each distribution must also be reported to Schwab via a DTCC AIP Activity and Distribution record (Record Type 050). This is in addition to, not instead of, the wire payment. Refer to DTCC-SOP-001 for the full DTCC AIP reporting process. Note: DTCC-SOP-001 is currently being updated and will be re-uploaded to Drive.
